What am I looking at?

The left panel shows the simulated classifier probabilities — one trace per state. A replay event is a sequence of broad, overlapping "reactivation" bumps, each lagged by the chosen time from the previous state (dotted lines mark event onsets). SODA was built for slow, overlapping responses (e.g. fMRI), so the bumps are wide by default.

The right panel shows the SODA slope dynamic. At every time point the state probabilities are regressed onto each state's position in the target sequence, giving one slope per time point (conventionally inverted ×−1). Averaged around event onset, a genuine overlapping sequence produces a biphasic curve: an onset period followed by an offset period. The grey band is a sequence-shuffle permutation null; the blue band is ±SE across events.

Try the presets (Fig. 6 right of Kern et al.): full overlap, only first & last item, single first/last item, scrambled order, and the little-overlap case where single-item onset/offset superpositions dominate.
